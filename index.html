Hello! My name is Sukyoung Choi. I am currently an assistant professor in the Department of Communication at Yonsei University. My research interests revolve around AI & communication technology and society, with an emphasis on quantitative methods.

I received my Ph.D. from the Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism at the University of Southern California. My dissertation on deepfakes in AI-mediated communication received the Herbert S. Dordick Dissertation Award from the International Communication Association (ICA), recognizing the most outstanding doctoral dissertation in the field of communication and technology completed and defended in 2023. Prior to joining USC Annenberg, I received my B.A. and M.A. in Communication from Seoul National University.

Research Themes

I study psychological and interpersonal processes in social media and online games, and how advances in AI impact mediated interaction. For game-based research, I was part of the Annenberg Game Lab, and I still collaborate with the team. I am also interested in exploring how the public makes sense of emerging technologies, such as AI and the metaverse, and their societal and political implications.

My work has been published in peer-reviewed journals, such as Communication Research, Computers in Human Behavior, Mass Communication & Society, Media Psychology, and New Media & Society.

A list of my publications is available at Google Scholar and ResearchGate.

Social Media Affordance & Identity

How does self-presentation on social media influence the way people view themselves? This project takes a deep dive into self-effects research by exploring the role of social media affordances in identity shift.

  • Publication

Social Media Use & Well-Being

Is social media use beneficial or detrimental to your well-being? What are the underlying mechanisms? Various methodologies (experiment, diary study, large-scale survey) have been used to explore these questions.

  • Publication

Online Games
& Social Interaction

This line of research is conducted in close partnership with game companies to explore how players communicate, interact, and play, and its effects on well-being and social capital.

  • Publication

Gameplay & COVID-19

Does gameplay help us cope with negative moods and social isolation during the pandemic? Do the pattern and effect differ across games? This project utilizes behavioral data and survey data from two games to address this question.

  • in progress

Public Understanding of AI

How does the public make sense of AI?
This project explores how various heuristics (e.g., media framing, emotion) affect support for AI development and regulation.
